# Cornell MPS in Information Science – Application Guide

---

## Program Overview

The **Master of Professional Studies (MPS) in Information Science** at Cornell University is a **one-year, professionally oriented graduate program** designed for students who want to pursue careers in industry rather than academic research.

The program is offered by the **Bowers College of Computing and Information Science** and focuses on the intersection of:

- Technology  
- Data  
- User experience  
- Social systems  
- Information design  

The MPS in Information Science emphasizes **real-world applications, hands-on projects, and interdisciplinary learning**.

---

## Program Duration and Structure

- The program is typically completed in **1 academic year** (full-time).
- The degree requires **30 credits** for the standard track.
- A **3-semester option (up to 39 credits)** is available for some students.
- There are **no fixed core courses** — students design their own study plan with advisor guidance.
- The program includes an **MPS Project (capstone project)**, often based on real-world industry or organizational problems.

---

## Who the Program Is Designed For

The MPS in Information Science is well-suited for applicants who:

- Have backgrounds in **computer science, data science, information systems, UX, design, engineering, or related fields**
- Want to pursue careers in:
  - Software & tech industry  
  - Data analysis  
  - UX / HCI  
  - Product and information systems  
- Prefer a **practical, career-focused graduate program**
- Want to develop **both technical and human-centered computing skills**

Applicants without formal CS degrees are still encouraged to apply **if they demonstrate sufficient technical, analytical, or quantitative preparation**.

---

## Academic Background Expectations

Although there is **no strict undergraduate major requirement**, successful applicants usually have experience in one or more of the following areas:

- Programming (e.g., Python, Java, R, C/C++)
- Data analysis
- Probability & statistics
- Linear algebra
- Calculus
- Information modeling or systems thinking

Applicants without strong technical preparation may find the coursework challenging.

---

## Application Eligibility

To apply, applicants must:

- Hold a **bachelor’s degree** or be in the final stage of completing one  
- Have a recommended **minimum GPA of 3.2 / 4.0**
- Meet **Cornell Graduate School English proficiency requirements** (for international students)

---

## English Language Proficiency (International Applicants)

International applicants must submit **TOEFL iBT or IELTS Academic** scores unless they qualify for an official waiver through previous English-language education.

Specific score thresholds are defined by the **Cornell Graduate School**, not solely by the MPS program.

---

## Standardized Tests (GRE)

- **The GRE is NOT required** for the MPS in Information Science.
- GRE scores are **not considered** in the admissions process.

---

## Required Application Materials

Applicants must submit the following:

- Online Application Form  
- Official Academic Transcripts  
- Resume / CV  
- Statement of Purpose (SOP)  
- Two Letters of Recommendation  
- Online Video Interview  
- English Proficiency Test Scores (if required)  

The admissions process follows a **holistic review** approach, meaning that academic background, professional experience, goals, and personal motivation are evaluated together.

---

## Admission Term and Timing

- The program primarily admits students for the **Fall semester**.
- Some applicants who are missing **1–8 undergraduate credits** at the time of application may still be considered on a case-by-case basis.
- Applicants are expected to complete all undergraduate degree requirements before enrollment.

---

## Curriculum and Learning Experience

Key characteristics of the MPS in Information Science curriculum include:

- **Flexible course selection** across information science, computing, data, design, and social systems
- Strong emphasis on **applied learning**
- A required **MPS Project** that integrates academic training with real-world problem-solving
- **Interdisciplinary collaboration** with students from diverse academic backgrounds

---

## Career Orientation

The MPS in Information Science is explicitly designed as a **professional degree**. Graduates commonly pursue careers in:

- Software engineering  
- Data and analytics  
- UX design & research  
- Product management  
- Information systems  
- Technology consulting  

The program focuses on preparing students for **industry practice rather than academic PhD pathways**.

---

## Summary for Applicants

The Cornell MPS in Information Science is a:

- One-year, professional graduate program  
- Focused on applied information science and technology  
- Designed for students seeking industry careers  
- Flexible in coursework  
- Project-based and interdisciplinary  
- GRE not required  
- Strong technical and analytical preparation recommended

# Frequently Asked Questions (for Chatbot Use)

### Q: Is the MPS in Information Science a research degree?  
A: No. It is a professional, career-oriented master’s program focused on applied industry skills.

---

### Q: How long does the program take to complete?  
A: Most students complete the program in one academic year.

---

### Q: Is the GRE required?  
A: No. The GRE is not required or considered.

---

### Q: Do I need a computer science background to apply?  
A: A formal CS major is not required, but technical, analytical, or quantitative preparation is strongly recommended.

---

### Q: What application materials are required?  
A: Applicants must submit transcripts, CV, Statement of Purpose, two recommendation letters, and complete an online video interview.

---

### Q: Is English proficiency required for international students?  
A: Yes. TOEFL or IELTS is required unless the applicant qualifies for a waiver.

---

### Q: What is the MPS Project?  
A: It is a capstone-style applied project that allows students to solve real-world problems using information science methods.
